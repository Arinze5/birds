Greater Rhea

AKA: Grey rhea or American rhea. Scientific Name: Rhea americana Family: Rheidae Species: R. americana Speed: 60 km/h Length: 127 – 140 cm Mass: 20 – 27 kg Wing Span: 150 – 210 cm Life Span: 15 years Location: South America Diet: Likes plants, fruits, and seeds, but also eats insects, lizards, small birds, and other small reptiles.

Appearance:

Has gray and light brown feathers. The head, neck, rump, and thighs are feathered. Males are darker than females. The head and bill are fairly small. The legs are long.

Facts:

  1. It is the largest of all South American large birds.
  2. If it feels there is a danger and it can’t defeat, it will fold its legs under itself and tuck down its head.
